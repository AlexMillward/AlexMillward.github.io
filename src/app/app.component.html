Alex Millward

Computer Scientist, Software Engineer

Associate, Securities Division, Goldman Sachs April 2018 onwards

I currently work within a technology function of the Prime Brokerage group in London, UK. My contributions are towards building, maintaining and enhancing systems for trading and trade management for the firm, its' clients and institutional peers. My role heavily involves development with Java, JavaScript and SQL, as well as proprietary technologies and platforms.


Associate, Core Infrastructure, Morgan Stanley August 2016 until April 2018

Whilst at Morgan Stanley, I worked in the Core Infrastructure division, building authentication technologies for the firm's client platform, and internal web portals. My team was responsible for frameworks offering both conventional, password-based authentication as well as a range of multi-factor authentication technologies. This work primarily involved a combination of Java and JavaScript programming, with LDAP as a data store, but also offered opportunities to work with C++ codebases. There was a strong emphasis on securing both cryptographic data and connections between systems, as well as architectural scale, stability and availability both within and outside of the firm.

My contributions were wide ranging, with particular focus on eliminating client-side vulnerabilities, maintaining and improving user experience, building out authentication workflows for new multi-factor technologies and improving the quality and rate of delivery by applying agile principles and implementing stronger testing and review practices.

From August to November 2018, I participated in the Technology Analyst Program, that provided technical training relevant to work in financial technology, and Morgan Stanley specifically. This included C++, Java, Scala, C#, UNIX, Relation Databases (SQL), Software Engineering and Web Technologies, as well as proprietary technologies and frameworks.


MComp, Computer Science, University of Sheffield September 2012 until June 2016

I received a first class Masters degree for my four year course in Computer Science at the University of Sheffield.

The final year of my course covered advanced topics, including the theory and practice of machine learning and high-parallelism computing with CUDA. A significant component of the year was a group project that my team and I undertook in collaboration with technical consultants at the UK Home Office, building a proof of concept system to automatically extract structured information from scans of documents associated with visa applications.

The first three years of my course provided in depth teaching on the theory of Computer Science, as well as broad-scoped teaching for programming (procedural, object-oriented, functional and logical), data modelling, 3D graphical procedures and APIs, computer security and machine learning (with a notable focus on text processing). During my third year, I combined interests in machine learning and economics for my dissertation in which I analysed the feasibility of using sentiment analysis techniques to generate an alternative measure of consumer confidence. The approach was evaluated against existing survey-based techniques, and the resulting report and presentation was awarded a first class grade.

Between my second and third years, I received funding from the University of Sheffield to work on a research project, investigating the use of agent-based modelling techniques to model microeconomic scenarios. I implemented a model of the interactions between small production firms, finding success in generating patterns of expansion and competitive interaction that would align with expectation given economic theory.
